Town Hall Meeting Devolves Into 45-Minute Argument About a Different Town

By The Daily Diss-patch Staff · July 22, 2026

SATIRE: This article is fiction and humor. Any resemblance to real persons or events is coincidental and played for laughs. Nothing here is news reporting or advice.

COCOA RIDGE, FL — What began as a routine city council meeting about a proposed stop sign on Weller Avenue transformed Tuesday night into a 45-minute argument about the parking situation in Palm Harbor Glen, a town none of the attendees live in, and which several attendees could not locate on the map provided.

The pivot occurred 12 minutes into the meeting, when resident Doug Kavanagh, 61, rose to comment on the stop sign and instead said the nine words that would consume the evening: 'You know who has this figured out? Palm Harbor Glen.'

'The second he said it, you could feel the room change,' said council member Rita Delgado, who has served for eleven years and described the meeting as 'top five, easily' for chaos. 'Half the room has never been to Palm Harbor Glen. The other half apparently goes there specifically to be angry.'

According to the meeting transcript, which runs 61 pages, attendees spent the next three quarters of an hour debating whether Palm Harbor Glen's angled downtown parking is 'genius' or 'a deathtrap,' whether its farmers market is 'charming' or 'smug,' and whether the town even qualifies as a town, given that, in the words of resident Carol Fentress, 'it's basically three streets and an attitude.'

Tempers peaked when Kavanagh's neighbor, Al Brzowski, produced printed photographs of Palm Harbor Glen's municipal flower beds and asked the council, 'Why can't we have this?' Delgado attempted to explain that Cocoa Ridge does have flower beds, gesturing toward the window, through which the flower beds were visible. Brzowski called the flower beds 'reactive rather than proactive' to sustained applause from a contingent in the back that officials later determined had come to the wrong meeting entirely.

The stop sign itself was addressed in the meeting's final ninety seconds, when the council voted 4-1 to 'look into it,' a designation that town historians note has previously absorbed 14 issues, none of which have re-emerged.

Reached for comment, Palm Harbor Glen's town manager expressed bewilderment at her municipality's role in the evening. 'People think we have it figured out?' she said. 'Our last council meeting was three hours on whether the new welcome sign font is too confident. A man cried. We are not the model. Nobody is the model. That's the whole secret of local government.'

Kavanagh, for his part, considers the meeting a triumph. 'For the first time in years, this community came together,' he said, loading his own printed photographs of Palm Harbor Glen into a folder labeled NEXT TIME. 'United by a common vision: that somewhere, 40 minutes away, things are slightly better. That vision is what democracy is all about.'

The Weller Avenue stop sign remains unbuilt. Palm Harbor Glen, informed of the debate, has reportedly scheduled its own meeting to discuss why Cocoa Ridge is talking about them, which residents there are calling 'classic Cocoa Ridge.'
